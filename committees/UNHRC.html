About the Committee

The United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC) is an intergovernmental body responsible for promoting and protecting human rights worldwide. Established in 2006, the Council addresses human rights violations, provides recommendations, and oversees the Universal Periodic Review (UPR) process, where countries are assessed on their human rights records. It holds regular sessions to discuss thematic issues such as freedom of expression, gender equality, and refugee rights. The Council aims to improve the global human rights landscape by addressing urgent violations, fostering dialogue, and supporting national and international efforts to uphold human dignity.
