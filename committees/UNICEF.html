About the Committee

UNICEF is a UN agency focused on improving the lives of children around the world. Founded in 1946, it works in over 190 countries to provide health care, education, and emergency relief. UNICEF advocates for children's rights, ensuring access to clean water, nutrition, education, and protection from violence and exploitation. The organization plays a crucial role in humanitarian crises, responding to disasters with child-focused aid. It collaborates with governments, non-governmental organizations, and communities to improve child welfare, prevent child labor, and promote early childhood development.
